====================================================================
PHISHING INDICATORS IDENTIFIED:
====================================================================

1. FAKE SENDER DOMAIN
   - Domain: shipment-tracking.delivery
   - Uses suspicious .delivery TLD
   - Not affiliated with FedEx, UPS, or legitimate carriers
   - Legitimate carriers use official domains (fedex.com, ups.com)

2. MALICIOUS URL (TYPOSQUATTING)
   - Link: fedx-delivery-update.com
   - Mimics "FedEx" but uses "fedx" (missing 'e')
   - Typosquatting technique
   - Domain likely registered recently

3. UNSOLICITED PACKAGE
   - Recipient not expecting delivery
   - No order confirmation reference
   - Creates curiosity to click
   - Common tactic for broad phishing campaigns

4. FINANCIAL THREAT
   - $25 "restocking fee" warning
   - 72-hour deadline
   - Creates pressure to act quickly
   - Bypasses verification instinct

5. GENERIC GREETING
   - "Dear Customer" (no personalization)
   - No recipient name or specific details
   - Legitimate carriers include customer name

6. FAKE TRACKING NUMBER
   - Format mimics real tracking numbers
   - Cannot be verified on official sites
   - Designed to appear authentic

====================================================================
ATTACK EXPLANATION:
====================================================================

This phishing scam exploits the prevalence of online shopping and 
package deliveries. The attack works because:

1. Many people regularly expect package deliveries
2. Curiosity prompts clicks even without ordering
3. Urgency and fees create pressure to act
4. Package tracking is routine and trusted

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU CLICK:

Scenario 1: CREDENTIAL THEFT
- Fake login page requesting email/password
- Credentials harvested and sold
- Used to compromise actual accounts

Scenario 2: PAYMENT FRAUD
- Fake "redelivery fee" payment form
- Requests credit card information
- Financial theft and identity fraud

Scenario 3: MALWARE DOWNLOAD
- Fake "tracking app" download
- Installs malware on device
- Data theft and system compromise

RECOMMENDED ACTION:
====================================================================

1. DO NOT click any links in the email
2. DO NOT enter any personal information
3. Verify tracking number on official carrier website
4. If no package expected, delete the email
5. Report to IT security if received on work email
6. Check credit card statements for unauthorized charges

HOW TO CHECK SAFELY:
1. Open new browser window
2. Type official carrier website directly (e.g., fedex.com)
3. Enter tracking number manually
4. Contact carrier customer service if concerns persist
